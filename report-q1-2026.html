physiological correlation

HRV (heart rate variability) is a stress proxy — higher is better, lower signals physiological strain. Your 3-month average is 55.8 ms, with a clear downward drift from January (avg 57.8) to April (avg 50.4). The lowest readings — 26.1 ms on April 22 and 33.7 ms on March 10 and 29 — correlate with high-intensity coding days. Resting heart rate mirrors this: it climbed from ~56 bpm in February to ~61 bpm in April.
